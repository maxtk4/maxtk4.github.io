My Projects

At Rice, I have spent most of my free time on various engineering design teams, mainly Rice Flight, the RC aircraft competition team run by the Rice Chapter of the American Insitute of Aeronautics and Astronautics, and the Rice Eclipse rocketry team.

Strix

Strix is a rocket flight controller I designed and built around an STM32 microcontroller to perform sensor fusion on accelerometer, altimeter, and gyroscope readings, as well as trigger in-flight events with 6 pyrotechnic channels.

Albatross

Albatross is the primary aircraft for the Rice Flight entry into the 2024 SAE Aero Advanced Competition. I designed all of the avionics for both the primary aircraft and the fully autonomous 'PADA's, small gliders designed to deploy from the primary aircraft in flight and autonomously land in target zones.

  • More

Magpie

The Rice Flight entry into the SAE Aero Advanced 2025 competition, a 3.5 pound fully autonomous aircraft capable of both conventional and vertical takeoff and landing. I am responsible for the avionics design of the craft, built around a Pixracer Pro flight controller and using a real-time-kinematic GPS system to enable centimeter-level precision while delivering and recovering payloads.

  • More

Alice: Custom UAV GCS Software

I designed and wrote a custom ground control station software to communicate with flight controllers inside aircraft built for the Rice Flight competition team. The software communicates using the open source MAVLink message set, which is widely used in commercial and hobbyist unmanned aerial vehicles.

  • More

Pigeon

Pigeon was a deployable quadcopter drone designed to deploy from Icarus and Daedalus, Rice Eclipse's two-stage entry into the 30 thousand foot category of the 2023 Spaceport America Cup. Pigeon would be released at an altitude of 1500 feet and autonomously navigate to a landing zone while recording geo-tagged images. I was responsible for designing and implementing the deploymnt system, which received a signal from an altimeter to release the drone.

  • More

Fastener Sorter

I worked with a team of three other people to design a sorter for mechanical fasteners. The device was based around a Raspberry Pi running a custom computer-vision algorithm written using Python and OpenCV, and used servo and stepper motors to manipulate and sort items.
